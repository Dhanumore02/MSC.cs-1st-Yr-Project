Types of Ice Creams

  • Vanilla
  • Butterscotch
  • Starwberry

Vanilla


Vanilla ice cream is very teasty ice creams. It is made with vanilla beans, milk, sugar, and cream. It is often considered to be a healthy dessert because it is made with natural ingredients. One cup of vanilla ice cream contains about 270 calories, 16 grams of fat, and 24 grams of sugar. It was originally created by cooling a mixture of cream, sugar, and vanilla above a container of ice and salt. The type of vanilla used to flavor ice cream varies by location.

Butterscotch


Butterscotch is such a classic flavor, it’s a wonder that butterscotch ice cream isn’t more mainstream. Butterscotch Ice Cream is very popular in India however we do not get it here in the US except at Indian ice cream stores. The butterscotch flavor in India is different from the butterscotch flavor in US. When I first moved to US, I remember getting very excited to find butterscotch chips and essence in grocery stores but when I tasted them I knew this was not what I was looking for.
Strawberry

It is made by blending in fresh strawberries or strawberry flavoring with the eggs, cream, vanilla, and sugar used to make ice cream. Most strawberry ice cream is colored pink or light red. Variations of strawberry ice cream include strawberry cheesecake ice cream and strawberry ripple ice cream, which is vanilla ice cream with a ribbon of strawberry jam or syrup.
